[cap.score]

[raise.title]
How the score goes up

[raise.body]
Your score is forest health: how much of the land is native forest.
It goes up when you bring the forest back.

Plant native forest on bare ground with "restore".
Pull young lantana so the natives take the ground back with "remove".

Your turn. Let us raise it.

[task1.header]
TASK 1. Raise the score.

[task1.prompt]
The glowing patch at {name} is bare ground. Plant native forest on it.

[task1.hint]
type: restore {name}   (or type skip)

[task1.badcell]
"{tok}" is not a cell on the map. Try {name}.

[task1.nochange]
Nothing changed there. Aim at the glowing patch: restore {name}

[task1.retype]
type: restore {name}

[task1.success]
Forest health rose from {before}% to {after}%. That is restoration. Bare ground became native forest.

[lose.title]
How the score goes down

[lose.body]
But lantana never quits. Left alone, it spreads into native forest.
Where it grows dense it turns into fuel, and one dry night it burns,
leaving bare ground behind. Both of those cost you score.

Let us watch it happen.

[task2.header]
TASK 2. Do nothing, and watch.

[task2.prompt]
Night {turn}, forest {health}%. Do not act. Just pass, and let lantana take its course.

[task2.pass]
press Enter to pass the night

[task2.result]
The forest fell from {start}% to {end}% while you watched. Lantana spread, and where it went dense, fire took the rest.
